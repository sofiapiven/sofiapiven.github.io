Welcome!


I am a postgraduate student at the University of Auckland with professional, teaching, and research assistant experience at the tertiary-level. I am an enthusiast for all things research, education, and digital.

My research ambitions concern quantitative and evolutionary anthropological approaches to understanding human cultural diversity.

I am specifically interested in the evolutionary, historical, social, and psychological "hows" and "whys" of what unites and distinguishes different "religions" - or that which approximates a coherent tradition of supernatural- or sacred-related behaviors and beliefs -, folklore, and more narrowly, works of literature, across space and time. Currently, I am pursuing a comparative project on Ancient Greek creation mythology and the Book of Genesis.

Next to my immediate research interests, I am a keen follower of tech journalism. I am interested in machine learning applications in academic research and what ML can offer for building learning environments for students.

Bachelor of Arts (Honours) student of anthropology at the University of Auckland, with an undergraduate background in biological anthropology and philosophy.
